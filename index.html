About Me

Hi! My name is Brian. I'm a Computer Science student at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI). I'm a Python aficionado, but still love getting down and dirty with more low level languages such as C and C++.

Notably, I am a mentor at the Rensselaer Center for Open Source (RCOS) and an undergraduate programming mentor for Computer Science I at RPI.

Education

I'm in the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute Class of 2017. Although I've only just completed my second year, as of May 2015, I have completed all but one of the required Computer Science courses in the curriculum. These courses include topics in Data Structures, Algorithms, Programming Languages, Discrete Mathematics, and Computer Organization.

Leadership

In RCOS, I provide assistance to projects including those I take a special interest in. There are a lot of procedures associated with being a successful project in the club, and over the past two years I've become familiar with all of them

As an undergraduate mentor for Computer Science I, I tutor students taking the class to help them understand all the concepts introduced in the class. Besides just simple ideas such as Boolean logic, functions, and some basic data structures, I also helped explain more abstract ideas such as Object Oriented thinking and recursion.
